Cisco Internship

Duration: May 2024 - July 2024

I successfully completed the Cisco Virtual Internship, where I tackled an exciting and challenging problem statement: Cyber Shield: Defending the Network. Here's a quick rundown of what I did, with a sprinkle of humor and professionalism:

Part 1: Securing the College Network

Tasks:

  • Campus Network Analysis: I examined my college's network like Sherlock Holmes, identifying devices, connections, and security controls.
  • Network Mapping: Used Cisco Packet Tracer to create a beautiful network diagram showcasing routers, switches, and firewalls.
  • Attack Surface Mapping: I played the role of a cyber detective, identifying potential vulnerabilities and proposing countermeasures to stop attackers in their tracks.

Deliverables:

  • A detailed network topology diagram (even my laptop seemed impressed!).
  • A security assessment report highlighting risks and offering solutions that made me feel like a cybersecurity superhero.

Part 2: Designing a Hybrid Working Environment

Scenario: My college needed a solution for faculty and students to access resources securely from both home and campus.

Tasks:

  • Explored the best network security products to create a hybrid working model.
  • Updated the campus topology to include these magical security components.
  • Proposed solutions that balanced security and convenience, proving that I’m not just about locking doors but opening them wisely.

Deliverables:

  • A shiny new topology with hybrid work-ready features.
  • Detailed reasoning for my design—because every decision deserves a story!

Part 3: Controlling Campus Internet Misuse

Scenario: Students were browsing irrelevant sites (probably looking for memes), and the college wanted to control web content access.

Tasks:

  • Researched how to block access to non-educational content (yes, no more cat videos during lectures).
  • Enhanced the topology with content filtering solutions.
  • Wrote simple and effective policies to restrict web categories, ensuring productivity and sanity for everyone.

Deliverables:

  • An updated topology that’s a fortress against distractions.
  • Easy-to-follow policies that even a bored student would understand.

Cloud Security: Protecting an E-commerce Platform

Scenario: A start-up needed help to handle traffic during flash sales and defend against DDoS attacks (think "mission impossible," but for the cloud).

Tasks:

  • Improved scalability and availability with smart designs—because nobody likes a crashing website during a sale!
  • Added backup services to prevent data loss during bottlenecks.
  • Designed a perimeter layer to fend off DDoS attacks like a cyber ninja.

Deliverables:

  • A revised architecture diagram that’s ready for both bursts of traffic and sneaky attackers.
  • A solid plan explaining how my design will save the day during high-pressure sales events.

Takeaway:

This internship was an amazing journey where I transformed into a network wizard and cloud architect. I learned how to analyze, design, and secure networks, all while keeping things practical and efficient. My deliverables didn’t just solve problems—they told stories of resilience, scalability, and cybersecurity awesomeness!

(P.S. Don’t worry, no networks were harmed in the making of this internship.)
